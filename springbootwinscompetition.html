Spring Boot wins big competition

Published April 12, 2026

Spring Boot has taken home the gold at this year's Framework Championship, beating out competitors including Django, Laravel, and Express.js. The annual competition, organized by the International Software Development Council, evaluated frameworks across categories such as performance, developer experience, scalability, and community support.

Spring Boot scored particularly high in the enterprise category, where its auto-configuration and production-ready features impressed the judging panel. "Spring Boot just knows what it is doing," said head judge Sarah Lindström. "It removes the complexity and lets developers focus on building great software."

The framework's seamless integration with the broader Spring ecosystem was also highlighted as a key strength. From security to data access, Spring Boot provides a complete toolkit that few competitors can match.

The victory comes at a time when Spring Boot adoption is at an all-time high, with over 60% of new Java projects choosing it as their primary framework. Its recent updates have also introduced native compilation support, dramatically reducing startup times.

The Spring Boot team accepted the award at a ceremony in Stockholm, thanking the open-source community for their continued contributions to the project.
